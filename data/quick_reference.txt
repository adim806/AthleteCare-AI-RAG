RECORD_TYPE: QA_PAIR | TOPIC: Return-to-play timelines

Q: How long does a Grade I hamstring strain take to return?
A: 5–10 days minimum. All criteria must be met regardless of time elapsed.

Q: How long does a Grade II hamstring strain take to return?
A: 3–6 weeks. Isokinetic dynamometer testing mandatory before Stage 4.

Q: How long does a Grade I calf strain take to return?
A: 5–10 days.

Q: How long does a Grade I ankle sprain take to return?
A: 5–7 days minimum.

Q: How long does a Grade II ankle sprain take to return?
A: 2–4 weeks.

Q: How long does a Grade I groin strain take to return?
A: 7–14 days.

Q: How long does ACL reconstruction rehabilitation take?
A: Return to training: 9–10 months. Competitive play: 10–12 months. Both are minimums.

Q: How long does a tibial stress fracture take to return?
A: Training: 10–14 weeks. Full competition: 14–18 weeks.

Q: What is the minimum return time after concussion?
A: 5 days minimum, following 6-step GRTP with 24 hours symptom-free between each step.

RECORD_TYPE: QA_PAIR | TOPIC: Acute injury management

Q: What is the POLICE protocol?
A: Protection, Optimal Loading, Ice, Compression, Elevation. Ice: 15–20 minutes every 2 hours for 48 hours.

Q: What must be avoided in the first 72 hours after a muscle strain?
A: Heat, massage, and direct stretching — these increase bleeding and delay healing.

Q: What does a Thompson test assess?
A: Achilles tendon integrity. Negative = Achilles intact.

Q: When is a Grade III injury referred to the physician?
A: Any Grade III or suspected surgical case = immediate Dr. Shapira referral.

RECORD_TYPE: QA_PAIR | TOPIC: RTP criteria and thresholds

Q: What LSI is required for Stage 4 and Stage 5?
A: ≥90% on single-leg hop test.

Q: What strength is required at Stage 3?
A: ≥80% of contralateral limb.

Q: What strength is required at Stage 4?
A: ≥90% of contralateral limb on isokinetic testing.

Q: What sprint speed is required for RTP?
A: ≥95% of pre-injury maximum on GPS.

Q: What is the ACL-RSI threshold?
A: ≥65/100 for psychological clearance.

Q: Can time alone determine RTP readiness?
A: No. All functional and psychological criteria must be met regardless of elapsed time.

RECORD_TYPE: QA_PAIR | TOPIC: Concussion

Q: Can a player with concussion return same day?
A: Never. Absolute rule — no exceptions.

Q: What assessment tool is used?
A: SCAT6 (Sport Concussion Assessment Tool, 6th Edition).

Q: How many concussions trigger neurologist referral?
A: 3 or more in a 3-year period. PLR-014 Dagan has 2 documented — one more = neurologist.
