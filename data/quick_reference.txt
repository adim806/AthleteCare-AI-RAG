RECORD_TYPE: QA_PAIR | TOPIC: Return-to-play timelines

Q: How long does a Grade I hamstring strain take to return?
A: 5–10 days minimum before return to full training, provided all criteria are met. Source: return_to_play.txt.

Q: How long does a Grade II hamstring strain take to return?
A: 3–6 weeks. Isokinetic dynamometer testing is mandatory before Stage 4 clearance. Source: return_to_play.txt.

Q: How long does a Grade I ankle sprain take to return?
A: 5–7 days minimum. Source: return_to_play.txt.

Q: How long does a Grade II ankle sprain take to return?
A: 2–4 weeks. Source: return_to_play.txt.

Q: How long does a Grade I groin strain take to return?
A: 7–14 days. Source: return_to_play.txt.

Q: How long does a Grade I calf strain take to return?
A: 5–10 days. Source: return_to_play.txt.

Q: How long does a Grade I knee MCL sprain take to return?
A: 1–2 weeks. Source: return_to_play.txt.

Q: How long does a Grade II knee MCL sprain take to return?
A: 4–8 weeks. Source: return_to_play.txt.

Q: How long does ACL reconstruction rehabilitation take?
A: Return to full training: 9–10 months. Return to competitive match play: 10–12 months. Both are minimums — all criteria must be met. Source: return_to_play.txt.

Q: How long does a tibial stress fracture take to return?
A: Return to training: 10–14 weeks. Return to full competition: 14–18 weeks. Source: return_to_play.txt.

Q: What is the minimum return time after concussion?
A: 5 days minimum, following the 6-step graduated concussion RTP protocol with 24 hours symptom-free between each step. Source: medical_staff_guide.txt.

RECORD_TYPE: QA_PAIR | TOPIC: Injury severity classification

Q: What is a Grade I muscle strain?
A: A mild injury with microscopic tearing, minimal functional loss, and expected return of 5–10 days.

Q: What is a Grade II muscle strain?
A: A moderate injury with partial tearing, significant functional impairment, and return of 2–6 weeks.

Q: What is a Grade III muscle strain?
A: A severe injury involving complete rupture. Return requires 3–9 months or longer.

Q: What is the difference between a Grade I and Grade II ankle sprain?
A: Grade I has no significant functional loss and returns in under a week. Grade II has partial ligament tearing, significant pain and swelling, and returns in 2–4 weeks.

RECORD_TYPE: QA_PAIR | TOPIC: Acute injury management

Q: What is the POLICE protocol?
A: Protection, Optimal Loading, Ice, Compression, Elevation. It is the standard first-response protocol for all acute soft tissue injuries at FC Velocity. Ice: 15–20 minutes every 2 hours for the first 48 hours.

Q: What must be avoided in the first 72 hours after a muscle strain?
A: Heat, massage, and direct stretching of the injured tissue — these increase bleeding and delay healing.

Q: When should a player be referred for imaging after an ankle injury?
A: X-ray to exclude fracture for all Grade II ankle sprains. MRI if ligament involvement is clinically suspected.

Q: When is a Grade III injury referred to the physician?
A: Any Grade III injury or suspected surgical case must be immediately referred to Dr. Anat Shapira.

Q: What does a Thompson test assess?
A: Achilles tendon integrity. A negative Thompson test confirms the Achilles tendon is intact.

RECORD_TYPE: QA_PAIR | TOPIC: Return-to-play criteria and thresholds

Q: What LSI is required for RTP Stage 4 and Stage 5 clearance?
A: Limb Symmetry Index ≥ 90% on the single-leg hop test.

Q: What strength level is required at RTP Stage 3?
A: Injured limb must reach at least 80% of the contralateral limb's strength.

Q: What strength level is required at RTP Stage 4?
A: Injured limb must reach at least 90% of the contralateral limb's strength on isokinetic testing.

Q: What sprint speed is required for RTP clearance?
A: At least 95% of the player's pre-injury maximum sprint speed on GPS measurement.

Q: What is the ACL-RSI threshold for psychological RTP clearance?
A: A score of 65 or above (0–100 scale). Below 65 = not psychologically cleared, regardless of physical results.

Q: Who must sign off on a Grade II injury RTP?
A: Both the responsible physiotherapist and Dr. Anat Shapira must sign off before the player advances to Stage 4.

Q: Can time alone determine return-to-play readiness?
A: No. Time alone does not determine readiness. All functional and psychological criteria must be met regardless of elapsed time.

RECORD_TYPE: QA_PAIR | TOPIC: Load management

Q: What ACWR range is considered safe?
A: 0.80–1.30.

Q: What ACWR indicates elevated injury risk?
A: 1.30–1.50 — elevated risk, requires monitoring.

Q: What ACWR indicates high injury risk?
A: Above 1.50. Tal Ben-David must immediately reduce the player's next session load and notify the physiotherapist.

Q: What GPS metrics are tracked daily?
A: Total distance, high-speed running (above 21 km/h), sprint distance (above 25 km/h), Player Load, and ACWR — via Catapult Sport GPS.

Q: What HRV drop triggers load management review?
A: A drop of more than 20% from personal pre-season baseline on consecutive mornings triggers a load management review with Tal Ben-David.

RECORD_TYPE: QA_PAIR | TOPIC: Prevention and warm-up

Q: Is the FIFA 11+ mandatory?
A: Yes. Mandatory before every training session and every match at FC Velocity.

Q: What does the FIFA 11+ reduce?
A: Hamstring injuries by up to 50%, ACL injuries by up to 40%, ankle sprains by up to 20% when performed consistently.

Q: What is the Nordic hamstring exercise protocol at FC Velocity?
A: 3 sets of 5–8 repetitions. Three times per week in the off-season. Twice per week in-season.

Q: What H:Q ratio indicates hamstring weakness requiring intervention?
A: H:Q below 0.55 triggers referral to a targeted hamstring strengthening programme.

Q: What sit-and-reach score triggers a flexibility programme?
A: Below 28 cm triggers enrolment in a daily posterior chain flexibility programme.

RECORD_TYPE: QA_PAIR | TOPIC: Concussion

Q: Can a player with suspected concussion return to play on the same day?
A: Never. Same-day return is absolute per UEFA regulations and FC Velocity policy — no exceptions.

Q: What assessment tool is used for concussion?
A: SCAT6 (Sport Concussion Assessment Tool, 6th Edition) — for all sideline and post-match assessments.

Q: What signs require immediate concussion assessment?
A: Loss of consciousness, confusion, headache, visual disturbance, or balance problems after a head impact.

Q: How many concussions in 3 years require neurologist referral?
A: 3 or more documented concussions in a 3-year period require neurologist referral before further RTP clearance.
