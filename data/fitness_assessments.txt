FC Velocity — Fitness Assessment Data and Standards

This is the single source of truth for all quantitative fitness data. Player IDs match the player profiles in players.txt.

---

ASSESSMENT SCHEDULE

Pre-season assessment: 3 weeks before the season starts. Full battery for all first-squad players.
Mid-season assessment: December winter break. Focus on fatigue monitoring and fitness changes.
Post-injury assessment: At RTP Stage 3 and Stage 4 to confirm functional readiness.
Annual comprehensive assessment: Includes blood panels, bone density for selected players, and psychological screening.

---

FITNESS TEST BATTERY AND BENCHMARKS

Yo-Yo Intermittent Recovery Test Level 2 (Yo-Yo IR2): Measures football-specific aerobic capacity. Benchmark outfield players: minimum 1080m, elite target 1400m+. Benchmark goalkeepers: minimum 780m.

30-metre sprint test (GPS timed): Measures acceleration and maximum speed. Benchmark outfield: below 4.0 seconds. Elite: below 3.8 seconds.

5-10-5 agility test (Pro Agility Shuttle): Measures lateral agility and change-of-direction speed. Benchmark outfield: below 4.4 seconds.

Single-leg hop test: Measures lower limb power and symmetry. Limb Symmetry Index (LSI) = injured side / uninjured side × 100. RTP threshold: LSI ≥ 90%.

Isokinetic dynamometry — quadriceps and hamstring: Peak torque measured at 60°/s. H:Q ratio benchmark: 0.60–0.70. Players with H:Q below 0.55 are placed on a targeted hamstring strengthening programme.

Sit-and-reach flexibility: Benchmark above 28 cm. Players below this threshold are placed on a daily hamstring and posterior chain flexibility programme.

Body composition (DXA scan or skinfold): Outfield benchmark 8–14% body fat. Goalkeeper benchmark 10–16%.

Resting heart rate (7-day average): Benchmark 42–58 bpm. Values above 60 bpm after a rest day may indicate insufficient recovery or illness.

Heart Rate Variability — HRV (RMSSD): Each player has a personal pre-season baseline. A drop of more than 20% from personal baseline on consecutive mornings triggers a load management review with Tal Ben-David.

LOAD MONITORING — ACWR POLICY

FC Velocity uses GPS tracking (Catapult Sport system) to monitor total distance, high-speed running (above 21 km/h), sprint distance (above 25 km/h), and Player Load daily.

The Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio (ACWR) is the primary injury risk indicator. ACWR is the ratio of the current week's training load to the rolling 4-week average. Safe zone: 0.80–1.30. Elevated risk: 1.30–1.50. High risk: above 1.50. When a player's ACWR exceeds 1.50, Tal Ben-David modifies their next session load and notifies the responsible physiotherapist immediately. ACWR data is reviewed every Monday morning at the medical and conditioning meeting.

NUTRITIONAL BENCHMARKS

Carbohydrate intake on match days: 8–10 g per kg of body weight.
Daily protein intake: 1.6–2.2 g per kg of body weight; post-training intake within 30 minutes is prioritised.
Hydration target: urine colour pale yellow (scale 1–3, PNSS chart) at the start of each session. Dehydration above 2% of body weight impairs sprint performance and increases injury risk.
Iron and Vitamin D: assessed twice per season. Deficiencies are common in professional footballers and are corrected with personalised supplement plans from nutritionist Daniela Ronen.
