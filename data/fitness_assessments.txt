RECORD_TYPE: FITNESS_POLICY
SECTION: Assessment schedule

Q: When does the pre-season fitness assessment take place?
A: 3 weeks before the season starts. Full battery for all first-squad players.

Q: When does the mid-season fitness assessment take place?
A: December winter break. Focus on fatigue monitoring and fitness changes.

Q: When does the post-injury fitness assessment take place?
A: At RTP Stage 3 and Stage 4 to confirm functional readiness for progression.

Q: What does the annual comprehensive assessment include?
A: Blood panels (including iron, Vitamin D, haematocrit), bone density for selected players, and psychological screening.

RECORD_TYPE: FITNESS_BENCHMARK
SECTION: Test battery benchmarks

Q: What is the Yo-Yo IR2 benchmark for outfield players?
A: Minimum 1080m. Elite target: 1400m+.

Q: What is the Yo-Yo IR2 benchmark for goalkeepers?
A: Minimum 780m.

Q: What is the 30-metre sprint benchmark for outfield players?
A: Below 4.0 seconds. Elite: below 3.8 seconds.

Q: What is the 5-10-5 agility benchmark for outfield players?
A: Below 4.4 seconds.

Q: What is the single-leg hop test RTP threshold?
A: Limb Symmetry Index (LSI) ≥ 90%. Calculated as: injured side distance ÷ uninjured side distance × 100.

Q: What is the isokinetic H:Q ratio benchmark?
A: 0.60–0.70 at 60°/s. H:Q below 0.55 triggers immediate referral to targeted hamstring strengthening programme.

Q: What sit-and-reach score triggers a flexibility programme?
A: Below 28 cm triggers enrolment in a daily posterior chain flexibility programme.

Q: What body fat benchmarks apply?
A: Outfield players: 8–14%. Goalkeepers: 10–16%.

Q: What resting heart rate is normal for FC Velocity players?
A: 42–58 bpm. Values above 60 bpm after a rest day may indicate insufficient recovery or illness.

Q: What HRV drop triggers a load management review?
A: A drop of more than 20% from a player's personal pre-season HRV baseline on consecutive mornings triggers a load management review with Tal Ben-David.

RECORD_TYPE: FITNESS_POLICY
SECTION: Load monitoring — ACWR

Q: What GPS system does FC Velocity use?
A: Catapult Sport GPS system.

Q: What GPS metrics are tracked daily?
A: Total distance, high-speed running distance (above 21 km/h), sprint distance (above 25 km/h), Player Load, and ACWR.

Q: How is ACWR calculated?
A: ACWR = current week's training load ÷ rolling 4-week average training load.

Q: What is the safe ACWR zone?
A: 0.80–1.30.

Q: What ACWR indicates elevated injury risk?
A: 1.30–1.50 — elevated risk, requires monitoring.

Q: What ACWR indicates high injury risk requiring immediate action?
A: Above 1.50 — Tal Ben-David must immediately modify the next session load and notify the responsible physiotherapist.

Q: When is the ACWR data reviewed?
A: Every Monday morning at the medical and conditioning meeting.

RECORD_TYPE: FITNESS_POLICY
SECTION: Nutritional benchmarks

Q: What is the carbohydrate intake target on match days?
A: 8–10 g per kg of body weight.

Q: What is the daily protein intake target?
A: 1.6–2.2 g per kg of body weight. Post-training intake within 30 minutes is prioritised.

Q: What is the hydration target before sessions?
A: Urine colour pale yellow (scale 1–3 on PNSS chart) at the start of each session.

Q: What level of dehydration affects performance?
A: Dehydration above 2% of body weight impairs sprint performance and increases injury risk.

Q: What micronutrients are assessed twice per season?
A: Iron and Vitamin D — deficiencies are common in professional footballers and corrected with personalised plans from nutritionist Daniela Ronen.
