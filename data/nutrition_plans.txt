RECORD_TYPE: NUTRITION_STANDARDS
SECTION: Squad-wide nutritional benchmarks

Q: What is the carbohydrate intake target on match days?
A: 8–10 g per kg of body weight. A 79 kg player (e.g. Alvarez) targets 632–790 g carbohydrate on match day.

Q: What is the daily protein intake target for players?
A: 1.6–2.2 g per kg of body weight per day. Post-training intake within 30 minutes is prioritised for muscle repair.

Q: What are the protein intake examples per player?
A: PLR-001 Alvarez (79 kg): 126–174 g/day. PLR-002 Ferretti (74 kg): 118–163 g/day. PLR-003 Volkov (89 kg): 142–196 g/day. PLR-004 Okafor (71 kg): 114–156 g/day. PLR-005 Becker (83 kg): 133–183 g/day.

Q: What is the hydration target before sessions?
A: Urine colour pale yellow (scale 1–3 on PNSS chart) at the start of each training session. Dehydration above 2% of body weight impairs sprint performance and significantly increases injury risk.

Q: What micronutrients are assessed twice per season?
A: Iron and Vitamin D. Deficiencies are common in professional footballers. Corrected with personalised plans from Daniela Ronen.

Q: What are the signs of iron deficiency that should be reported?
A: Persistent fatigue despite adequate rest, reduced training performance, pallor, shortness of breath during exercise. Refer for blood panel if any 2+ signs present.

Q: What is Eitan Levi's nutritional protocol?

Q: What foods are emphasised in Eitan Levi's plan?
A: Dairy: 3+ servings/day (milk, yoghurt, cheese). Fortified plant-based alternatives if dairy is tolerated poorly. Leafy greens: kale, broccoli, pak choi at minimum one meal/day. Fatty fish: 2+ servings/week for Vitamin D (salmon, mackerel, sardines).

Q: When is Eitan Levi's nutritional follow-up?
A: January 2025 — blood panel for calcium, Vitamin D (25-OH-D3), and bone density DXA scan.

Q: What Vitamin D level is being targeted for Eitan Levi?
A: Target serum 25-OH-D3: >75 nmol/L (from deficient level at injury). Values below 50 nmol/L indicate deficiency requiring treatment.

RECORD_TYPE: NUTRITION_POLICY
SECTION: Supplement approval process

Q: What is the process for approving a player supplement at FC Velocity?
A: Step 1 — Player or staff requests supplement approval. Step 2 — Daniela Ronen checks supplement against the WADA prohibited list and reviews ingredients. Step 3 — Dr. Shapira gives medical clearance. Step 4 — Player provides written informed consent. Step 5 — Supplement is documented in KineticMind. No supplement may be administered without all 5 steps completed.

Q: Which supplements are currently approved for the squad?
A: Vitamin D (Levi — deficiency treatment), calcium (Levi — bone health), fish oil/Omega-3 (Volkov — anti-inflammatory, WADA-approved), protein supplements post-training (squad-wide, WADA-checked brands only). All others require individual review.

Q: Is creatine permitted at FC Velocity?
A: Creatine is not on the WADA prohibited list. However, any use requires approval by Daniela Ronen and Dr. Shapira with documented player consent. No blanket approval — individual review required.

RECORD_TYPE: NUTRITION_POLICY
SECTION: Match day nutrition protocol

Q: What is the pre-match meal protocol?
A: Main pre-match meal: 3–4 hours before kick-off. High carbohydrate (2–3 g/kg), moderate protein (0.3 g/kg), low fat and fibre to avoid GI disturbance. Examples: pasta with chicken or fish, rice with vegetables and lean protein. Snack 1–2 hours before kick-off: easily digestible carbohydrate, low fibre. Examples: banana, toast with honey, sports drink.

Q: What is the half-time nutrition protocol?
A: Carbohydrate intake at half-time for matches lasting 90+ minutes: 30–60 g carbohydrate. Sports drink, banana, or energy gel (WADA-checked brand only). Water available throughout.

Q: What is the post-match recovery nutrition protocol?
A: Within 30 minutes of final whistle: 1.0–1.2 g/kg carbohydrate + 20–25 g protein. Recovery meal within 2 hours. For evening matches: protein-rich meal before sleep (casein protein or dairy — e.g. cottage cheese) to support overnight muscle repair.

RECORD_TYPE: NUTRITION_POLICY
SECTION: Weight and body composition management

Q: What are the body fat benchmarks at FC Velocity?
A: Outfield players: 8–14%. Goalkeepers: 10–16%. Players outside range are referred to Daniela Ronen for individual review.

Q: What is the approach if a player is above the body fat benchmark?
A: Gradual reduction only — no crash dieting. Target 0.5 kg per week maximum loss to preserve muscle mass. Caloric deficit achieved through carbohydrate moderation, not protein reduction. Daniela Ronen monitors weekly.

Q: Is rapid weight cutting permitted before matches?
A: No. Rapid dehydration or weight cutting is prohibited. Dehydration above 2% of body weight impairs sprint performance and increases injury risk — this is incompatible with performance standards.
