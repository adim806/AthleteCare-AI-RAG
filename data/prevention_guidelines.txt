FC Velocity — Injury Prevention Guidelines

Injury prevention is the highest-priority function of the FC Velocity medical department. Most football injuries are not random accidents — they are predictable events reducible through systematic preparation. Teams with structured prevention programmes experience 25–50% fewer muscle injuries than those without (UEFA Health and Performance Project data).

---

FIFA 11+ WARM-UP (mandatory before every session and match)

The FIFA 11+ programme is compulsory at FC Velocity, supervised by the physiotherapists and Tal Ben-David. It consists of three parts. Part 1 (8 minutes): running exercises with hip flexion, hip abduction, shoulder contact, and hopping. Part 2 (10 minutes): six strengthening exercises in three sets each — Nordic hamstring curls, single-leg squats, Copenhagen planks, lateral band walks, plank with arm raise, and side-to-side hops with landing control. Part 3 (2 minutes): moderate and high-speed directional running. The FIFA 11+ reduces hamstring injuries by up to 50%, ACL injuries by up to 40%, and ankle sprains by up to 20% when performed consistently.

---

HAMSTRING INJURY PREVENTION

Hamstring injuries are the most common injury in professional football. The primary prevention tool is the Nordic hamstring exercise (Russian hamstring curl): player kneels with feet anchored and lowers their torso forward using eccentric hamstring contraction. Protocol: 3 sets of 5–8 reps, three times per week in the off-season, twice per week in-season. A 2015 Cochrane review found the Nordic exercise reduces hamstring injuries by 51%.

Players with a sit-and-reach score below 28 cm are placed on a daily 10-minute hamstring stretching routine. Players with any previous hamstring injury perform an additional 10-minute individual warm-up before joining team training — this is a permanent requirement. Marco Alvarez (PLR-001) is on this enhanced hamstring protocol due to his 2022 Grade II and 2023 Grade I injuries.

---

ACL INJURY PREVENTION

High-risk indicators for ACL injury: poor knee valgus control on landing, H:Q ratio below 0.55, high sprint volume with a limited strength base, and previous ACL injury (the strongest individual risk factor). Prevention exercises include plyometric landing training with video feedback on knee valgus angle, single-leg stability work (Bulgarian split squats, single-leg RDLs, wobble board), hip abductor strengthening (lateral band walks, clamshells), and the Copenhagen plank series. Luca Ferretti (PLR-002) is enrolled in the permanent ACL prevention programme following his 2023 reconstruction.

---

PRE-SEASON MUSCULOSKELETAL SCREENING

Every player undergoes full screening before the start of each season. Screening components: Functional Movement Screen (FMS) — 7 patterns scored 0–3, any score of 0 or 1 is flagged for remediation. Isokinetic strength test at 60°/s and 240°/s. Hip range of motion assessment — asymmetry above 10° triggers a hip mobility programme. Ankle dorsiflexion test — below 10° indicates elevated ACL and Achilles risk. Landing mechanics video analysis — knee valgus angle and trunk inclination assessed on drop jump. Players with multiple risk factors are enrolled in a targeted individual prevention programme before the season starts.

LOAD MANAGEMENT

The safe ACWR range is 0.80–1.30. ACWR above 1.50 signals high injury risk. Full load management policy and ACWR calculation details are defined in fitness_assessments.txt. During fixture congestion (three or more matches in seven days) the medical team holds a daily 10-minute briefing to assess squad load.

---

RETURN FROM INTERNATIONAL DUTY

Players returning from international duty submit their training and match data from the national team. Tal Ben-David reviews the load and may impose a reduced training load for the first 3–5 days. Players who have crossed more than 3 time zones are placed on a jet lag recovery protocol.

---

HEAT AND HYDRATION POLICY

During training above 28°C: water breaks every 15 minutes, electrolyte drink provided at the half-time break of sessions over 60 minutes. Training must not continue in WBGT conditions above 32°C without Dr. Shapira's approval. A 2% body mass loss through dehydration impairs sprint performance and significantly increases injury risk.
