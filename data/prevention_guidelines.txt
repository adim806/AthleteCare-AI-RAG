FC Velocity — Injury Prevention Guidelines
SOURCE: prevention_guidelines.txt | DOMAIN: prevention | UPDATED: November 2024
CHUNK_FORMAT: PREVENTION_ENTRY | Each section is self-contained and independently retrievable.

CORE PRINCIPLE: Most football injuries are not random accidents — they are predictable events reducible through systematic preparation. Teams with structured prevention programmes experience 25–50% fewer muscle injuries than those without (UEFA Health and Performance Project data).

RECORD_TYPE: PREVENTION_PROGRAMME
PROGRAMME: FIFA 11+ Warm-Up | MANDATORY: Yes — before every session and every match

Q: Is the FIFA 11+ warm-up mandatory at FC Velocity?
A: Yes. The FIFA 11+ programme is compulsory before every training session and every match. Supervised by the physiotherapists and Tal Ben-David.

Q: What does Part 1 of the FIFA 11+ warm-up include?
A: Part 1 (8 minutes): running exercises with hip flexion, hip abduction, shoulder contact, and hopping.

Q: What does Part 2 of the FIFA 11+ warm-up include?
A: Part 2 (10 minutes): six strengthening exercises, three sets each — Nordic hamstring curls, single-leg squats, Copenhagen planks, lateral band walks, plank with arm raise, and side-to-side hops with landing control.

Q: What does Part 3 of the FIFA 11+ warm-up include?
A: Part 3 (2 minutes): moderate and high-speed directional running.

Q: How effective is the FIFA 11+ at preventing injuries?
A: When performed consistently, the FIFA 11+ reduces hamstring injuries by up to 50%, ACL injuries by up to 40%, and ankle sprains by up to 20%.

RECORD_TYPE: PREVENTION_PROGRAMME
PROGRAMME: Hamstring Injury Prevention | PRIORITY: Highest — most common injury in professional football

Q: What is the primary prevention tool for hamstring injuries at FC Velocity?
A: The Nordic hamstring exercise (Russian hamstring curl). The player kneels with feet anchored and lowers their torso forward using eccentric hamstring contraction.

Q: What is the Nordic hamstring exercise protocol at FC Velocity?
A: 3 sets of 5–8 repetitions. Three times per week in the off-season. Twice per week in-season.

Q: How effective is the Nordic hamstring exercise?
A: A 2015 Cochrane review found the Nordic exercise reduces hamstring injuries by 51%.

Q: What triggers a daily hamstring stretching routine?
A: A sit-and-reach score below 28 cm places the player on a daily 10-minute hamstring stretching routine.

Q: What is the enhanced hamstring warm-up policy for players with previous hamstring injury?
A: Players with any previous hamstring injury perform an additional 10-minute individual warm-up before joining team training. This is a permanent, non-negotiable requirement.

Q: Which player is currently on the enhanced hamstring protocol?
A: Marco Alvarez (PLR-001) — due to 2022 left hamstring Grade II strain and 2023 right hamstring Grade I strain (bilateral history). The enhanced warm-up protocol has been in place since August 2022.

RECORD_TYPE: PREVENTION_PROGRAMME
PROGRAMME: ACL Injury Prevention | PRIORITY: High — reconstruction has 10–12 month recovery

Q: What are the high-risk indicators for ACL injury at FC Velocity?
A: Poor knee valgus control on landing, H:Q ratio below 0.55, high sprint volume with a limited strength base, and previous ACL injury (the strongest individual risk factor).

Q: What exercises are used for ACL injury prevention?
A: Plyometric landing training with video feedback on knee valgus angle, single-leg stability work (Bulgarian split squats, single-leg RDLs, wobble board), hip abductor strengthening (lateral band walks, clamshells), and the Copenhagen plank series.

Q: Which player is on the permanent ACL prevention programme?
A: Luca Ferretti (PLR-002) — enrolled following his March 2023 ACL reconstruction. Programme includes: twice-weekly plyometric landing sessions, weekly isokinetic strength assessment, and enhanced FIFA 11+ variant. This is a permanent, lifelong programme.

RECORD_TYPE: PREVENTION_PROGRAMME
PROGRAMME: Pre-Season Musculoskeletal Screening | FREQUENCY: Before every season

Q: What does the FC Velocity pre-season musculoskeletal screening include?
A: Functional Movement Screen (FMS) — 7 movement patterns scored 0–3 (any 0 or 1 flagged for remediation). Isokinetic strength test at 60°/s and 240°/s. Hip ROM assessment (asymmetry >10° triggers hip mobility programme). Ankle dorsiflexion test (below 10° indicates elevated ACL and Achilles risk). Landing mechanics video analysis (knee valgus angle and trunk inclination on drop jump).

Q: What FMS score triggers a remediation programme?
A: Any score of 0 or 1 on the Functional Movement Screen (FMS) is flagged for a targeted remediation programme.

Q: What hip ROM asymmetry triggers intervention?
A: Asymmetry above 10° between hips triggers a hip mobility programme.

Q: What ankle dorsiflexion threshold indicates elevated injury risk?
A: Below 10° of ankle dorsiflexion indicates elevated risk for ACL and Achilles tendon injuries.

Q: What happens if a player has multiple risk factors on pre-season screening?
A: Players with multiple risk factors are enrolled in a targeted individual prevention programme before the season starts.

RECORD_TYPE: PREVENTION_POLICY
SECTION: Load management

Q: What is the safe ACWR zone at FC Velocity?
A: 0.80–1.30.

Q: What ACWR indicates elevated injury risk?
A: 1.30–1.50 indicates elevated risk requiring load monitoring.

Q: What ACWR indicates high injury risk requiring immediate action?
A: Above 1.50. Tal Ben-David must immediately reduce the player's next session load and notify the responsible physiotherapist.

Q: What protocol applies during fixture congestion (3+ matches in 7 days)?
A: The medical team holds a daily 10-minute briefing to assess squad load, with particular attention to ACWR values and HRV trends.

---

RECORD_TYPE: PREVENTION_POLICY
SECTION: Return from international duty

Q: What happens when a player returns from international duty?
A: The player submits training and match data from the national team. Tal Ben-David reviews the load and may impose a reduced training load for the first 3–5 days. Players who have crossed more than 3 time zones are placed on a jet lag recovery protocol.

RECORD_TYPE: PREVENTION_POLICY
SECTION: Heat and hydration policy

Q: What hydration protocol applies during training above 28°C?
A: Water breaks every 15 minutes. Electrolyte drink provided at the half-time break of sessions over 60 minutes.

Q: What WBGT temperature stops training?
A: Training must not continue in WBGT conditions above 32°C without Dr. Shapira's approval.

Q: What level of dehydration impairs performance and increases injury risk?
A: A 2% body mass loss through dehydration impairs sprint performance and significantly increases injury risk.
